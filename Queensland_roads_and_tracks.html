Description

Abstract / Description:
Queensland Roads and Tracks (QRT) is the Department of Resources’ foundation dataset of roads data. It is a vector (line segment) dataset depicting the approximal centreline location and attributes of roads and tracks across Queensland. It does not replicate a navigable product.

The Data Dictionary is published here https://www.resources.qld.gov.au/__data/assets/pdf_file/0003/1592310/roads-tracks-data-dictionary.pdf
Purpose:
The Department of Resources foundation roads dataset. It is a vector (line segment) dataset of approximal centreline location and attributes of roads and tracks across Queensland.
Resource specific usage:
Usage: Public
Additional Information:
For the purposes for which The Department of Resources collate roads information, a road is considered that which has a discrete name and is continuously navigable. This includes State Controlled Roads, Local Government Controlled Roads, Local Roads of Regional Significance, Private Roads, Tracks and driveable beaches.
The dataset also includes some bikeways, walkways, ferry routes and busways. The dataset comprises road segments for each section of a Road between nodes, intersections or road features, represented by a line between two vertices. The data contains persistent road IDs so that each road has a consistent id, as well as persistent identifiers for each road segment. Other attribution includes street naming information, road classification, surface type, trafficability and road usage.
The open data product excludes segments that have been made historic. Segments may be made historic for permanent road closures or for data management purposes.
This dataset replaced the Baseline Roads and Tracks (BRAT) dataset.

History:

Lineage statement:
The Queensland Roads and Tracks (QRT) dataset is a replacement for the Baseline Roads and Tracks (BRAT) dataset, with richer attribution. The BRAT data was mapped across into the new QRT schema in September 2020. The open data format of QRT was first released in November 2021.
Road segments inserts and geometry and/or attribute updates are triggered when there are changes to the spatial cadastre, or changes made in data provided by Local Government, Department of Transport and Main Roads, Queensland Parks and Wildlife Service, or other external parties to the Resources roads team.
Geometry and some attributes are also validated against aerial imagery and other resources by the Resources roads team.
The point of truth for names is updated from the authority responsible for the road.
Where roads do not have a specific name, standardised generic terms have been utilised such as ‘road’, ‘track’ etc. All segments of roundabouts have been named as ‘roundabout’.
Process step:
Road segments inserts and geometry and/or attribute updates are triggered when there are changes to the spatial cadastre, or changes made in data provided by Local Government, Department of Transport and Main Roads, Queensland Parks and Wildlife Service, or other external parties to the Resources roads team.
Segments are assigned their locality and Local Government Areas (LGA) attributes based on a spatial join with PROP.QLD_LOCALITYBDY_DCDB and PROP.QLD_LOCGOVBDY_DCDB datasets. Currently, when a road segment spans multiple localities or LGAs, the location of the longest portion of the segment is used to determine these fields.
Source:
Sources used in the maintenance of the dataset include the Queensland spatial cadastre, Local Government, Department of Transport and Main Roads, Queensland Parks and Wildlife Service, other relevant State Government Departments, aerial imagery, changes to the Resources addressing database, aerial imagery, and user feedback.
